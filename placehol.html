Talents

Talents 0:50

MoP replaced the old 40-point talent trees with six rows you can swap per boss. Here's the breakdown:

  • Row 1 (15): Pure utility — doesn't affect DPS. On fights like Zorlok, untalent it entirely so you can't grief the raid if mind controlled.
  • Row 2 (30): Angelic Feather — always. Non-negotiable. See below.
  • Row 3 (45): Solace & Insanity is the default (best single-target). Mindbender for cleave fights where you stand still (e.g. Stone Guard). From Darkness, Comes Light for movement-heavy encounters — gives you something to cast on the move.
  • Row 4 (60): Angelic Bulwark is the default — a substantial HP buffer when you get low. Switch to Desperate Prayer on Amber-Shaper (Bulwark's shield scales with the construct and you'll get a 2M shield — don't be that guy).
  • Row 5 (75): Twist of Fate if you can get at least 50% uptime (fights with snipeable adds = more Shadow Orbs + flat bonus damage). Otherwise Power Infusion for flat single-target fights (Feng, Blade Lord) — line one up with your opener, one with execute phase. Divine Insight is behind this phase.
  • Row 6 (90): See Level 90 Talents below.

Why Angelic Feather? 4:52

The MoP version of Angelic Feather is dramatically better than retail:

  • 80% movement speed (double retail)
  • 6 second duration (1 second longer)
  • 10 second cooldown (half retail)

It's hard to not have it available. You go from being a wheelchair class to having near-permanent sprint uptime. Macro it to @player to skip the targeting reticle and cast it instantly at your feet.

Body and Soul is worse because a healing priest will be shielding you anyway, and if the shield is on cooldown when you actually need the speed, you're stuck.

Level 90 Talents 5:55

  • Halo — default choice. Must be at least 20 yards from the target for full damage. Use this WeakAura to track your distance.
  • Divine Star — take this if your strategy forces you to stack in melee, or when farming celestial dungeons.
  • Cascade — only for Elegon and Galon at this point.

Stats

Stats 6:35

Item level wins 9 times out of 10 — the raw stamina, intellect, and secondary stats across 18 slots add up to more than chasing a perfect stat spread. But never at the expense of hitting these key thresholds:

  1. Hit cap — 15%. Hit rating, Spirit (converts passively), and Expertise all count toward this. Once you're capped, reforge/regem away from it.
  2. Haste — to 8085 rating. The most important breakpoint for our DOTs. See below.
  3. Crit ≥ Mastery. Crit is slightly ahead in T14. This relationship ping-pongs as new tier sets and items become available.

How our DOTs work 7:54

Our three main DOTs — Shadow Word: Pain, Vampiric Touch, and Devouring Plague — all have fixed durations. Within that window, they tick a set number of times.

More haste does three things: reduces cast times, reduces your GCD, and adds extra ticks within the same duration. For example, Devouring Plague might do 8 ticks instead of 6 in the same 6-second window. That's free damage.

Haste Breakpoints 8:28

You get 5% haste baseline from Shadowform. The first breakpoint is trivial to hit.

The one that matters is 8085 haste rating — this gives 2 extra ticks of Shadow Word: Pain and 2 extra ticks of Devouring Plague. Building and spending orbs as often as possible is the name of the game, so those extra DP ticks are a huge deal.

Later breakpoints: 30% is achievable with raid gear. 14,000 and 18,000 rating become reachable in Throne of Thunder and Siege of Orgrimmar. Use the Haste Calculator to plan your gearing.

T14 4pc Haste Breakpoints 9:35

The Tier 14 4-piece bonus extends both Shadow Word: Pain and Vampiric Touch by 3 seconds, giving you a free extra tick by default. This changes the breakpoint values — VT adopts the same breakpoint formula as SWP.

8085 remains the key target. Sim your character to check whether chasing the next breakpoint beyond that is worth the intellect you'd sacrifice.

Gems 10:05

  • Early gearing: Quick Sun's Radiance (yellow) and Energized Wild Jade (blue) to reach 8085 as fast as possible. Reforging and enchants help too.
  • Once 8085 is hit naturally: Swap to Reckless Vermilion Onyx and Purified Imperial Amethyst for a better intellect/secondary balance.

If simming shows that chasing the next breakpoint costs too much intellect, stop stacking haste and go for the balanced gems.

Stat Scaling 10:43

Comparing Phase 1 to Phase 5 gear: weapons get double the spell power, double the stamina, massive intellect gains, and socket bonuses. Rings see similar jumps. Multiply that across 18 slots and scaling classes like Shadow Priest and Fire Mage become monsters by the end of the expansion.

The tradeoff: you may feel behind early on, but as you hit higher breakpoints and GCD cap later, it pays off.

Rotation

Opener 11:34

  1. Stand at least 28 yards from the boss.
  2. At 2.5s on the pull timer, cast Halo — the travel time makes it land on zero.
  3. At 1s, use Potion of the Jade Serpent.
  4. Cast Mind Spike — this procs passive trinkets and Jade Spirit.
  5. Pop cooldowns: Berserking / Power Infusion + Engineering gloves.
  6. Apply Vampiric Touch → Shadow Word: Pain → Devouring Plague. Your first round of DOTs snapshots all your active buffs.
  7. Mind Blast on cooldown (always building orbs).
  8. Spam Mind Flay: Insanity.

Shadowfiend in MoP dynamically updates with Bloodlust after spawning — no more begging for lust before you cast it.

Spell Priority 13:23

Everything revolves around building and spending Shadow Orbs:

  1. Devouring Plague — only at 3 orbs.
  2. Mind Blast — on cooldown, always.
  3. Shadow Word: Death — in execute or to snipe adds for orbs + Twist of Fate.
  4. Mind Flay: Insanity — channel as many as possible after DP.
  5. DOT refresh — keep SWP and VT rolling. Ensure at least 8 seconds remain before casting DP so you don't need to refresh mid-insanity.
  6. From Darkness, Comes Light procs — use on the move.
  7. Halo — if 20+ yards away.
  8. Mind Flay — filler. Still packs a punch, but interrupt it for anything above.

Plan ahead: the entire priority revolves around not bottlenecking yourself. If your dots fall off while you're channelling insanity, you're trolling your own damage.

Mind Flay: Insanity 15:11

Devouring Plague buffs Mind Flay: Insanity by 33% per orb spent. At 3 orbs (which is always), that's 100% bonus damage on every MFI channel.

Snapshot trick: recast MFI just before Devouring Plague expires. The channel will snapshot and retain the full 100% buff for its entire duration, even after DP falls off. This is a meaningful DPS gain.

To compare yourself to other Shadow Priests: look at their total MFI damage relative to how many Devouring Plagues they cast. That ratio tells you how well they're converting orbs into insanity damage.

Macros 16:35

Macros combine multiple button presses into one and smooth out the gameplay. Key macros to set up:

Cooldowns

Berserking + Power Infusion (if talented) + Engineering gloves + trinkets all in one press. Works great with double passive trinkets since there's no cooldown conflict.

#showtooltip
/cast Berserking
/cast Power Infusion
/use 10
/use 13
/use 14

Shadowfiend

Casts Shadowfiend, auto-targets a living enemy if needed, and commands the pet to attack.

#showtooltip
/cast Shadowfiend
/cleartarget [dead][noharm]
/targetenemy [noexists]
/petattack

Shadow Word: Death

Auto-targets a living enemy if your current target is dead or doesn't exist. For example, on Heroic Lei Shen when Ball Lightnings spawn you Mind Sear the pack then spam this macro — it automatically retargets a living add and fires Shadow Word: Death to generate orbs and do big damage. Far quicker than tab-targeting or clicking manually.

#showtooltip Shadow Word: Death
/cleartarget [dead][noharm]
/targetenemy [noexists]
/cast [stance] Shadow Word: Death; [nostance] Shadowform
/cast Shadow Word: Death

Level 90 Talent

One button for Cascade, Divine Star, or Halo — automatically casts whichever you have talented.

#showtooltip
/cast [talent:6/1]Cascade
/cast [talent:6/2]Divine Star
/cast [talent:6/3]Halo

Dispersion

First press activates, second press cancels — like a paladin bubble.

#showtooltip Dispersion
/cast Dispersion
/cancelaura Dispersion

Angelic Feather

Cast at your feet with @player to remove the targeting reticle entirely. Instant 80% speed boost whenever you need it.

#showtooltip
/cast [@player] Angelic Feather
